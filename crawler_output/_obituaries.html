    Obituaries

    Highlights

    1. Daniel J. Berrigan, Defiant Priest Who Preached Pacifism, Dies at 94

      Father Berrigan, a Jesuit priest and poet, defiantly protested the Vietnam War and served time for his acts of civil disobedience.

      By DANIEL LEWIS

    2. Samuel Billy Kyles, Witness to King’s Last Moments, Dies at 81

      Mr. Kyles was on the balcony of a Memphis motel with the Rev. Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. when the civil rights leader was gunned down.

      By SAM ROBERTS

      1. Insider Podcasts

        The Last Word: Inside the Times’ Obits Department

        The editor of the Obituaries section, William McDonald, talks about “Obit,” the movie and obit, the job.

        By SUSAN LEHMAN

      2. Notable Deaths 2016

        Remembering Nancy Reagan, David Bowie, Harper Lee, Merle Haggard, Anita Brookner, Clyde Lovellette, Anne Jackson, Monte Irvin, Maurice White, Umberto Eco and others who died this year.

        By THE NEW YORK TIMES

    1. Remo Belli, Developer of the Synthetic Drumhead, Dies at 88

      Mr. Belli’s Weather King, so named because it was impervious to humid conditions, largely replaced animal hides and helped feed rock ’n’ roll fever.

      By SAM ROBERTS

    2. Blackie Sherrod, 96, Texan Who Wrote About Sports With an Informed Swagger, Dies

      Mr. Sherrod, voted the state’s best 16 times, was admired for his lively style and for mentoring Dan Jenkins and other younger writers.

      By BRUCE WEBER

    3. Philip Kives, Pitchman Who Perfected ‘As Seen on TV’ Infomercials, Dies at 87

      Mr. Kives’s company, K-tel, saturated airwaves for decades with ads for gadgets like the Veg-O-Matic and compilation albums like “Hit Machine.”

      By MARGALIT FOX

    4. Tommy Kono, Weight-Lifting Champion Raised in Internment Camp, Dies at 85

      Kono learned to lift as a child in a Japanese-American detention camp during World War II and went on to win two Olympic gold medals in the sport.

      By FRANK LITSKY

    5. George Weymouth, Conservationist, Horse Enthusiast and Bon Vivant, Dies at 79

      Mr. Weymouth, a member of the du Pont family, was a longtime fixture in fox-hunting, polo, steeplechase and carriage-driving circles.

      By WILLIAM GRIMES

    Latest Articles

    1. Ed Davender, Former Kentucky Basketball Stalwart, Dies at 49

      Davender ranked 11th in Kentucky scoring with 1,637 points and eighth with 436 assists while playing with the Wildcats from 1984-88.
      April 30, 2016

    2. Willie L. Williams, Head of 2 Police Forces in Turmoil, Dies at 72

      Mr. Williams was the first African-American to lead the police in Philadelphia, where he made progress, and in Los Angeles, where he faced conflicts.

      By BRUCE WEBER
      April 28, 2016

    3. Jenny Diski, Author Who Wrote of Madness and Isolation, Dies at 68

      Ms. Diski’s novels included “Skating to Antarctica,“ “Stranger on a Train: Daydreaming and Smoking Around America With Interruptions” and “Rainforest.”

      By WILLIAM GRIMES
      April 28, 2016

    4. Robert Linton, Steadfast ’80s Wall Street Banker, Dies at 90

      Mr. Linton’s Drexel firm pioneered the junk bond industry and came to symbolize the era’s “greed is good” mentality, until it was swept into a federal inquiry.

      By BEN PROTESS
      April 28, 2016

    5. Winston Hill, Durable Lineman for ’69 Champion Jets, Is Dead at 74

      Hill made eight All-Pro teams and protected the blindside of quarterback Joe Namath during the team’s only Super Bowl victory.

      By DANIEL VICTOR
      April 27, 2016

    6. Ozzie Silna, Savvy A.B.A Owner Who Got Rich Off the N.B.A., Dies at 83

      A businessman who made millions off N.B.A. television contracts long after the team he co-owned with his brother, Daniel, was dissolved.

      By RICHARD SANDOMIR
      April 27, 2016

    7. Robert J. Cooper, 39, Creator of Popular Elderflower Liqueur, Dies

      Mr. Cooper’s 2007 concoction, St-Germain, was so embraced by the cocktail crowd that it became known as “bartender’s ketchup.”

      By ROBERT SIMONSON
      April 27, 2016

    8. Harry Wu, Who Told World of Abuses in China, Dies at 79

      Mr. Wu was brutalized for 19 years in Communist labor camps, and later returned to his homeland undercover to expose prison conditions.

      By SAM ROBERTS
      April 27, 2016

    9. Horace Ward, U.S. Judge Who Triumphed Over Bias, Dies at 88

      Mr. Ward sued the University of Georgia after it rejected his law school application because of his race. He became Georgia’s first black federal judge.

      By SAM ROBERTS
      April 27, 2016

    10. Les Waas, Adman, Dies at 94; Gave Mister Softee a Soundtrack

      His jingle, a herald of summer in the city, and of the ice cream man down the block, is the textbook embodiment of an earworm: once heard, never forgotten.

      By MARGALIT FOX
      April 27, 2016

    2. Richard Lyons, 57, a Founder of the Subversive Band Negativland, Dies

      Since the late 1970s, Mr. Lyons’s subversive media-hacking band has functioned more like a collective with a fluctuating membership.

      By BEN RATLIFF
      April 27, 2016

    3. Madeleine Sherwood, 93, Actress on Stage, Film and ‘Flying Nun,’ Dies

      A Canadian who originated the Broadway role of Abigail in Arthur Miller’s “The Crucible,” she also starred in stage and film versions of Tennessee Williams plays.

      By SAM ROBERTS
      April 26, 2016

    4. Maurice Kenny, Who Explored His Mohawk Heritage in Poetry, Dies at 86

      Mr. Kenny gave voice in his verse to historical figures of New York State, both white and Native American.

      By JOHN MOTYKA
      April 26, 2016

    5. Billy Paul, Singer of the Hit ‘Me and Mrs. Jones,’ Dies at 81

      Mr. Paul’s 1972 ballad about an illicit romance was a success that he was unable to repeat in his later career.

      By WILLIAM GRIMES
      April 25, 2016

    6. Papa Wemba, Congolese King of ‘Rumba Rock,’ Is Dead at 66

      A supple tenor who sang danceable fusions of African pop and world music, Papa Wemba performed in his native Lingala and in French.

      By MARGALIT FOX
      April 25, 2016

    7. Qi Benyu, Mao Aide Unrepentant About Cultural Revolution, Dies at 84

      Mr. Qi, a propagandist in the Chinese Communist Party, expressed no remorse about the brutalities under Mao Zedong and remained a steadfast admirer of him.

      By CHRIS BUCKLEY
      April 25, 2016

    8. Walter Kohn, Who Won Nobel in Chemistry, Dies at 93

      A chemist and physicist who fled Nazi-occupied Vienna as a teenager and built a distinguished academic career in the United States, becoming an American citizen in 1957.

      By SAM ROBERTS
      April 25, 2016

    9. Banharn Silpa-archa, Former Prime Minister of Thailand, Dies at 83

      Mr. Banharn’s brief tenure, in 1995 and 1996, was marked by allegations of corruption and economic mismanagement.

      By SETH MYDANS
      April 24, 2016

    10. Elton Spitzer, 84, Who Helped Turn WLIR Into a Radio Destination, Dies

      Mr. Spitzer, who operated 92.7 FM on Long Island, dared to be different by introducing listeners to new wave music most Americans hadn’t heard before.

      By BRUCE WEBER
      April 24, 2016

    11. Papa Wemba, Known as the King of Congolese Rumba, Dies at 66

      He rose to fame in the 1970s with the band Zaiko Langa Langa, which inspired a generation of African musicians.

      By THE ASSOCIATED PRESS
      April 24, 2016
