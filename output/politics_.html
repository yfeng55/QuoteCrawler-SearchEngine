    Highlights From Buffettpalooza

    The annual shareholder meeting of Berkshire Hathaway has been called the Woodstock of capitalism, drawing tens of thousands of investors from all over the world. And for good reason, because the gathering here in Berkshire’s hometown serves as a celebration of Warren E. Buffett and his decades-long career as one of the best-known investors ever.

    DealBook reported on the conference from the CenturyLink Center.

    How to Do Deals the Berkshire Way

    Mr. Buffett is revered as a consummate deal maker, one who is famous for striking huge acquisitions after seemingly brief periods of due diligence and closing with a handshake.

    But, Mr. Sorkin of DealBook asks, will that work in the future once someone else takes the reins at Berkshire?

    What followed might as well be the Tao of Buffett, or how to do a deal the Berkshire way. The most important thing that matters isn’t negotiating every fine point, but being right on the broader prospects of the potential takeover target.

    “The mistakes are always about making an improper assessment of the economic future,” he says.

    Berkshire has made some mistakes over the years, but Mr. Buffett says that none would have been fixed with a little more due diligence. Instead, they would have been avoided by being smarter about analyzing the business’ future.

    It dovetails with his answer to a previous question about Berkshire companies making acquisitions of their own. The billionaire criticized one made by Lubrizol, a big chemical manufacturer, describing the deal simply as one that had been struck as oil prices began to fall.

    (Speculation on Twitter points to Lubrizol’s purchase of the global oil field chemicals business Weatherford, a deal struck in 2014 and closed in 2015.)

    “We have made some decent acquisitions there, but the biggest should not have been made,” he says.

    Just as important is making sure that one trusts the party across the table. Mr. Munger interjects that the “human quality of the management team” is crucial.
